## Market Overview

The business process documentation tools market is estimated at **~$2 billion** in 2025 (Business Research Company), growing at **~11-12% CAGR**, projected to reach **$3.5-4B by 2032**. Note: various market research firms give wildly different numbers ($1.5B to $15B) depending on how broadly they define the category.

**What's driving growth:**
- Remote/hybrid work → need for async documentation
- Digital transformation across enterprises
- Compliance and audit requirements
- Employee onboarding at scale

**What's threatening it:**
- AI agents that can *execute* processes instead of *documenting* them
- LLMs that can generate documentation from descriptions alone
- Screen recording becoming commoditized (built into OS, free tools everywhere)

**Who's winning:** Scribe is the clear market leader in the "auto-capture step-by-step guides" niche. They just hit a **$1.3B valuation** (Nov 2025). WalkMe and Whatfix dominate enterprise digital adoption ($79K+ avg annual contracts). Loom (acquired by Atlassian) dominates video messaging.

## The AI Agent Threat — The Elephant in the Room

Let me be brutally honest. This section is the most important in this entire document.

### The core question: If AI agents can DO the process, who needs documentation of HOW to do it?

**The timeline:**
- **Today (Feb 2026):** AI agents (Claude Computer Use, OpenAI Operator, Google Mariner) can navigate websites, click buttons, fill forms, and complete multi-step workflows. They're not perfect — maybe 60-70% reliable on complex tasks — but they're improving fast.
- **1 year (Feb 2027):** AI agents will reliably handle 80-90% of routine digital workflows. "Click here, then type this, then click submit" processes become fully automatable.
- **3 years (2029):** AI agents will be standard in enterprise software. Most repetitive processes won't need human execution at all.
- **5 years (2031):** Process documentation for routine digital tasks will be as quaint as writing directions to a restaurant when GPS exists.

### What happens to Scribe, Tango, etc.?

They face the **exact same existential threat.** Why document "how to submit an expense report in SAP" when an AI agent can just… do it for you?

Scribe seems to understand this — their pivot to "Optimize" (process mining and intelligence) is telling. They're moving from *"document the process"* to *"understand and improve the process."* That's smart. They see the writing on the wall.

### Is there still a market for how-to guides?

**Yes, but it's shrinking and shifting:**

1. **Compliance & audit:** Regulated industries need documented procedures regardless of whether humans or AI execute them. "We need a record of the process" survives even when agents execute it.
2. **Training & learning:** People still need to *understand* processes even if agents can do them. You don't want a company where nobody knows how things work and the AI is a black box.
3. **Edge cases & non-digital processes:** Physical processes, complex judgment calls, creative workflows — these resist agent automation.
4. **Process design:** Someone still needs to *design* the process the agent will follow. Documentation of intent, not just steps.
5. **Agent instructions:** Ironically, AI agents may need structured process descriptions to know what to do. The format changes, but "process knowledge" still matters.

### But let's be real:

The **volume** of process documentation needed will **collapse** as agents take over routine tasks. The market that Scribe, Tango, and Ondoki serve today — "capture my clicks and make a guide" — will shrink by 50-70% within 3-5 years for digital workflows.

The surviving market will be:
- **Process intelligence** (understand & optimize, not just document)
- **Agent workflow design** (creating processes FOR agents to follow)
- **Compliance documentation** (proving how things should be done)
- **Complex/physical processes** (things agents can't do)

### Where does Ondoki fit?

Honestly? **Ondoki is in a tough spot.** You're a small player in a market that's about to get disrupted by AI agents, competing against a $1.3B-valued leader (Scribe) who has already started pivoting.

### Potential pivots that could save the product:

1. **"Process Intelligence for SMBs"** — What Scribe Optimize does for enterprise, do for small teams. Capture processes, then use AI to identify inefficiencies, suggest automations, benchmark against best practices.

2. **"Agent Workflow Builder"** — Pivot from "document what humans do" to "design what agents should do." Build a visual process designer that outputs agent-executable workflows (MCP servers, API calls, browser automation scripts).

3. **"Process Compliance Platform"** — Focus on regulated industries (healthcare, finance, manufacturing) where documented SOPs are legally required regardless of who/what executes them.

4. **"Picks and shovels" play — Process capture SDK/API** — Instead of being the end product, become the *infrastructure* that other tools use to capture processes. Sell the capture engine to agent platforms, CRM vendors, LMS providers.

5. **"Living Documentation"** — Documentation that *stays in sync* with the actual application. When the UI changes, the docs auto-update. This is the #1 pain point with process docs today — they go stale immediately.

## How Ondoki Can Win (or at least survive)

### The Play: Open Source Scribe

Scribe spent $30M+ educating the market that "auto-capture process documentation" is a thing. Now be the open-source version. This playbook has worked repeatedly:

| Proprietary | Open Source Alt | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Notion | AppFlowy | Growing, funded |
| Slack | Mattermost | $100M+ ARR |
| Figma | Penpot | Growing, funded |
| Airtable | NocoDB | Growing, funded |
| Zapier | n8n | $40M+ funding |
| Intercom | Chatwoot | Growing, funded |
| **Scribe** | **Nobody (yet)** | **The gap** |

### What to build next (prioritized):

1. **🔴 Open source the repo.** Pick a license (AGPLv3 like n8n/Mattermost is the safe bet — prevents SaaS reselling without contributing back). Get on GitHub. Write a README that says "open-source alternative to Scribe." This IS the differentiation.

2. **🔴 Self-hosted story.** Docker Compose one-liner, Helm chart, Railway/Render deploy button. This is what sells to enterprises who can't use SaaS.

3. **🔴 Chrome Extension on Web Store.** This is your PLG funnel. Someone Googles "open source Scribe alternative", finds you, installs extension, starts capturing.

4. **🟡 Community.** GitHub Discussions or Discord. Contributor guide. Public roadmap. The community IS the moat for open source.

5. **🟡 Process analytics.** Don't just capture — analyze. "Your team documented 47 processes. 12 are stale. Average process takes 23 steps — industry benchmark is 15." This differentiates from pure capture tools AND future-proofs against agents.

### What to STOP building:
- **Desktop apps.** You have 6 abandoned attempts. Pick ONE (Electron) or none. Browser extension + web app is enough to start.
- **Knowledge base/wiki.** Export to Notion/Confluence instead. Don't rebuild what exists.
- **Video documentation.** Loom/Guidde own that. Stay focused on step-by-step guides.

### The AI agent angle — not a threat, an opportunity:
- Agents need structured process definitions to know what to do
- Ondoki-captured processes → agent-executable workflows is a natural evolution
- "Record the process once, let the agent replay it forever" — THAT's a product

### Go-to-market:
- **SEO:** "open source Scribe alternative", "self-hosted process documentation", "free Scribe alternative" — these are uncontested keywords
- **Product Hunt launch** once self-hosted story is ready
- **GitHub stars** as social proof (the open source GTM flywheel)
- **r/selfhosted** community — they actively look for open-source SaaS alternatives

- **Paid:** Desktop capture, exports (PDF/MD), analytics, team collaboration, branding, AI features, API access

## Honest Assessment

### Can Ondoki compete? The brutal truth.

**As a closed-source SaaS competing head-to-head with Scribe:** No. Don't even try.

**As the open-source alternative to Scribe:** Yes, absolutely. The playbook is proven (n8n, Mattermost, NocoDB, Chatwoot, Penpot), the gap is real (nobody has done it), and Scribe's $1.3B valuation actually HELPS you by proving market demand.

**The AI agent threat still applies** — but it applies equally to Scribe. And open source + self-hosted has an advantage here: you can pivot faster, the community can build agent integrations, and enterprises who need compliance documentation will prefer self-hosted regardless.

### The realistic outcomes (reframed for open source play):

1. **🟢 Open source growth (45%):** Ondoki becomes the go-to open-source Scribe alternative. Grows organically via GitHub, r/selfhosted, SEO. Builds a community. Revenue from hosted version + enterprise features (SSO, audit, support). Think: NocoDB, Chatwoot trajectory.

2. **🟡 Niche + open source (25%):** Ondoki finds a vertical (healthcare, compliance) AND is open source. Smaller market but higher margins and stickier customers.

3. **🟢 Acquisition (15%):** A larger company (Atlassian, Notion, GitLab) acquires the open-source project for the technology and community. Open source projects get acquired at higher multiples than closed-source alternatives.

4. **🔴 Doesn't launch (15%):** The open-sourcing never happens, or the product stays in permanent beta. This is the real risk — not competition, but shipping.

### The one thing that matters most:

**Ship it open source.** The wedge isn't a feature. The wedge is: "Scribe, but you own your data." Every other decision — pricing, features, go-to-market — flows from this.

Dubble is a side project from a consultancy. CaptureToDoc has 2 commits. Nobody is doing this. The window is open, but it won't stay open forever.
